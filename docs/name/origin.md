# Verso

## Where the name comes from

---

We wanted to name the thing that journaling actually is — not the writing, but the mental act underneath it. The word we kept returning to was **contemplation**: directing attention inward, deliberately, as a practice.

We looked at how different traditions have talked about this — Greek, Sanskrit, Daoist, Sufi, Jewish, Christian. They differ in important ways, but they share a family resemblance: the idea that turning inward is something you *do*, not something that happens to you.

That's journaling. Not recording your day. Thinking by writing. Turning something over until it changes shape.

**Verso** comes from the Latin *versare*: to turn over in the mind. It shares a root with *verse* — a line of writing, which originally meant a turn of the plow at the end of a furrow. You drive forward, reach the edge, and turn back to work the next row. One line, then another. That's what a journal entry is. The mind working a field.

We picked it because it's short, it sounds right, and it doesn't need to overexplain itself.

Verso. Turn it over.
